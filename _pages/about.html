In the AI-SENDS (AI-based Sensing, Networking, and Data Services) Lab, we develop intelligent systems that perceive, communicate, reason, and act cooperatively in uncertain, distributed, and resource-constrained environments. Our research focuses on AI-native agents—from autonomous vehicles and drones to edge devices and assistive systems—that learn the environment, anticipate the consequences of actions, and make decisions under networking, sensing, and computational constraints. A unifying theme across our work is diversity-aware and communication-aware learning, bridging information theory, machine learning, and large/vision language models (LLMs/VLMs) to enable coordinated autonomy at scale. We pursue this vision through tightly coupled theory-driven and application-driven research, working closely with domain experts in transportation, robotics, biomedical imaging, public safety, and industrial systems. Representative research directions include:

  • Prediction-based and semantic communication for autonomous systems under bandwidth and uncertainty constraints
  • Cooperative perception and collective intelligence augmented by multi-agent sensing for extended visibility and safety-aware autonomy
  • Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) and diversity-aware modeling for biomedical imaging and biosignal analysis
  • Diversity-aware learning frameworks for distributed and federated systems, grounded in information-theoretic principles
  • AI-enabled aerial robots with overhead manipulators augmented by learning-based perception and reasoning for operation in complex environments
  • Micro- and nano-scale 3D reconstruction from digital holographic recording using deep learning methods

These projects involve concepts from information theory, graph-based learning, deep and reinforcement learning, LLM/VLM-based multimodal reasoning, rate–distortion theory, semantic communication, and adversarial training.

These projects are supported by NSF, US Air Force Research Laboratory, USDA, BMW Research Group, MIT Lincoln Laboratory, Clemson’s VIPR Project (DoD), SC EPSCoR, and Arizona Commerce Authority. For more information, check out my Research Page and feel free to send me an email at arazi@clemson.edu.
